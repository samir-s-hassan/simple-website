PEDRI

Biography

    Early Life

Pedro González López was born in Bajamar, Tenerife, Canary Islands in Spain. When he was only 3 years old, his family moved to Tegueste, a nearby town. As a youngster, he was fascinated with soccer or as he calls it "fútbol." He joined UD Tegueste, starting as a centre-back for the club before he could even read properly. Never into traditional studies, his parents would find him with a football at his foot or sometimes even in his mouth. When he turned 12 and understood life isn't only about football, he got rejected by his first crush. Her exact words were "you dumb footballer, you'll never make any money." So to get back at her he joined CF Juventud Laguna, where he played until 2018. Luckily, Lehigh University came calling and his life's been different ever since.

    Life at Lehigh

At Lehigh, Pedri became one of the college's best soccer players. Moving swiftly through the midfield in game and in real life, everyone wanted to be Pedri. Freshmen bought Pedri toothbrushes. Sophomores changed their names to Pedri on the Lehigh name change form. Juniors started protesting Lehigh's other athletic teams by throwing hard-boiled eggs at the players during the game and calling it "The Pedri". No one knows why but it's funny. Seniors were too jealous of the young Spaniard stealing their loves so they stopped banned the letter "P" as a sign of disrespect. Though, everything turned normal when they found out Pedri received a B- in MATH 081. "Come on it's not even real math" echoed across the streets everytime Pedri stepped foot outside. It proved to be too much for the young magician as he decided to move back to his homeland. And that was all she wrote (he kicked).

    Projected Life in Spain

Very high expectations in Spain for Pedri. News sources say the young baller is expected to score 11 goals in 67 games. The Spanish monarchy hopes for him to get 0 goals in 18 appearances for the national team. Which actually isn't that much. Expected from the Spanish monarchy.
